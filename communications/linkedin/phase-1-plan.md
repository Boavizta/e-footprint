## Pacing rationale

- **W1 = meta intro, W11 = re-plan tease.** Bookend posts. The intro builds audience attention upfront (a "subscribe to this thread" signal); the closer closes the loop and primes the next session.
- **W2–W5 mirror Article #1's structure** (intuition → best practices → measurement → simulation). Each is self-contained but the sequence builds toward the Jul 6 launch. By W6 the audience already has the conceptual frame; the article confirms rather than introduces.
- **Template-reference CTAs in W2–W5** address the "5 abstract-thinking posts in a row" risk. Each foundation post invites the reader to open a template in the interface and see the concept *concretely*. This presupposes Step 6 of the tutorial-and-documentation work is shipped — see dependencies below.
- **W7 + W8 = the two July posts.** W7 leverages Article #1's most quotable line as a standalone post. W8 launches Article #2. Two posts in July matches the slow-down model.
- **August silence is real.** Resist the temptation to "just one post". Audiences understand summer breaks; persistence in August produces lower engagement and burns the cadence.
- **September posts (#9–#11) all derive from Article #2.** Article #2 launched in late July; September derivatives reference its permanent URL. The 6-week gap between launch and first derivative is acceptable because the article URL is permanent — the September posts simply re-surface it for readers who didn't catch the July launch.
